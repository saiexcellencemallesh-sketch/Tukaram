Sant Tukaram Maharaj

"Truth is the only freedom; untruth is bondage."

The Voice of Bhakti

Sant Tukaram Maharaj (17th century) was a revolutionary Marathi saint-poet who brought spiritual wisdom to the masses through his Abhangs. A devotee of Lord Vitthala, he famously chose the "eternal wealth" of devotion over his worldly profession as a trader and moneylender.

Era17th Century Maharashtra, India.
Primary DeityLord Vitthala (Panduranga), a form of Lord Krishna.
Literary WorkOver 4,500 Abhangs compiled in the Tukaram Gatha.
Key LegacyMaking spiritual knowledge accessible in the vernacular Marathi language.

What Did Tuka Pray?

"O Panduranga, society calls me mad for abandoning my business. But they do not know I have found the imperishable wealth of your name. You freed me from worldly traps so I could become one with You".

The Insight: Tukaram interpreted the loss of his worldly business not as ruin, but as divine liberation. He cast away temporary, monetary wealth to gain the eternal wealth of devotion.

Analogy: A hot air balloonist cuts away heavy sandbags. To those on the ground, he is throwing away valuables; to the pilot, it is the only way to soar higher.

Core Teachings

  • Internal Purity: Emphasized the purification of the heart (Antahkaran) through kirtan and chanting God's name.
  • Detachment: Taught that worldly goods—even basic clothing—are temporary and will not accompany the soul after death.
  • Finding Grace: Encouraged seeing every circumstance, including adversity, as a manifestation of God's grace.
  • Universal Service: Preached liberation through loving service to mankind and constant remembrance of the Divine.
